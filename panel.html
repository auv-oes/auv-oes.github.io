Panel discussion
UN Ocean Decade: Marine Robotics and Autonomous Sensing for a sustainable ocean



The UN Decade of Ocean Science for Sustainable Development (2021-2030, known as the Ocean Decade) is generating increasing enthusiasm and energy in the ocean science communities.

Marine robotics and autonomous systems community have a big part to play in taking technology forward in the coming Decade for improved sensing and Ocean exploration.

As part of the Decade, ten challenges have been outlined for humanity to tackle in the coming years. Marine robotics and autonomous sensing systems can contribute directly to challenges 1, 3, 4, 7 and 9, but indirectly has an impact on all nine challenges.

The panel discussion will focus around the Decade theme, namely, how to achieve the "Science we Need for the Ocean we Want", in the context of marine autonomy. It may span sub-topics such as:
  • The importance of marine robotics and autonomous sensing systems during the Decade, with particular relevance to individual challenges.
  • how autonomy can contribute towards sustainable use and exploration of the Ocean, and contribute to economic growth.
  • How to get the private sector, academia, government, philanthropic organizations to work harmoniously towards accelerating solutions in marine robotics and autonomy and remove impediments to development.
Moderator: Richard Mills, Kongsberg Maritime
Panelists:
  • John Potter, Norwegian University of Science and Technology.
  • William Kirkwood, Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute.
  • Neil Bose, Memorial University of Newfoundland.
  • Mark Roberts, Kongsberg Maritime.

Format:
The session will be hybrid and open, allowing virtual participation to non-registered participants as well. The Zoom link will be made available closer to the event.
The session will open with each panelist getting an opportunity to share their views on the topic. At the end of the opening talks, the floor will be opened to the audience for Q&A by the moderator. Total panel duration 90 minutes.

Date: 19 September

Time: 17:30 - 19:00, SGT

Venue: Shaw Alumni House, National University of Singapore, and virtually accessible via Zoom to all participants, via this link.
Starting from 18th, participants can open the app at https://pigeonhole.at/AUVSINGAPORE , and either upvote existing questions, or comment on what additional questions you would like to include. Alternatively, in-person attendees may ask questions orally during the session, while virtual attendees can ask via the Zoom chat, and it will be included on the pigeonhole list. The panel moderator will take the final call on what questions will be asked, depending on the course of the discussion.
